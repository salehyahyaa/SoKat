SpaceScan

Measure a rectangular closet, pantry, or alcove from one iPhone photo — with honest, validated results.

  1. Take one photo of the whole closet opening — floor, back wall, and ceiling line.
  2. Tap its 6 corners following the on-screen diagram; the app checks the geometry before it computes anything.
  3. Enter one measurement you know (like the ceiling height) — that sets the scale for the rest.

Supported: box-shaped enclosed spaces — closets, pantries, alcoves.
Not supported: couches, chairs, irregular furniture, open rooms — the geometry assumes a rectangular floor and a vertical back wall, and scans that don't fit are rejected.

Runs entirely on this phone — no install, no server.

The 6 corners you'll tap

  1. 1 back-bottom-left
  2. 2 back-bottom-right
  3. 3 front-bottom-left
  4. 4 front-bottom-right
  5. 5 back-top-left
  6. 6 back-top-right
  • Show the entire opening: floor, rear wall, and ceiling line
  • Stand centered, phone at chest height, avoid severe tilt
  • Prefer the 1× lens (avoid 0.5× ultra-wide when possible)
  • Good lighting; every corner you'll tap must be visible

One photo — the whole closet

Photograph the closet so the floor's front edge, the back wall, and the ceiling line are all in frame.

Stand centered at a natural height. If it doesn't fit, step back rather than switching to the 0.5× lens.
